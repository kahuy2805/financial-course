Lessons for Your Trading

Lesson 1: Pattern Recognition Must Be Precise

The inverse head and shoulders pattern was textbook:

  • Both shoulders at similar levels
  • Head clearly lower
  • Neckline as entry trigger
  • Volume confirmation

Before you enter a technical trade, ask:

  • [ ] Can I describe the pattern to someone else clearly?
  • [ ] Does price confirm at each support level?
  • [ ] Is volume aligned (declining on selling, rising on buying)?
  • [ ] Can I define the stop loss based on the pattern?

Lesson 2: Stop Losses Aren't Optional

James set his stop at $45.48 BEFORE entering. He never moved it.

  • Entry: $47.23
  • Stop: $45.48 (3.7% below entry)
  • If stopped, knows loss is $2,250 (3% of account)
  • Stops losses aren't "loss," they're "max-loss-definition"

Your rule: Every trade needs a stop loss defined BEFORE entry.

Lesson 3: Scaling Out Locks In Wins

James's actual strategy (revised):

  • Hold 100% until first target ($50.20)
  • Sell 30% there to lock in profits
  • Hold 70% until $60
  • Sell 50% at $60 to lock in more
  • Hold 20% for the "home run"

This strategy:

  • Guarantees you keep some profits
  • Lets you participate in larger moves
  • Reduces emotional pressure
  • Provides certainty

Lesson 4: Catalysts Shorten Timeframe

Without earnings on April 27, this could have been a multi-month hold.

WITH earnings coming:

  • Time window compressed to 16 days
  • Binary risk defined (beat or miss)
  • Exit plan straightforward (sell before announcement)
  • Position sizing stayed the same but context changed

When trading catalysts:

  • Know the date
  • Don't hold through if your thesis is just "earnings bounce"
  • Move quickly in the days before the event

Risk: $2,250 (defined stop)

Reward: $24,011 (actual gain)

Ratio: 10.7:1



This is not average. Most trades aim for 2:1 or 3:1.

3. Catalyst was coming (defined upside event)

4. Timing was right (bounce right after support tested)

  • Been wrong early: -5% (could have happened)

His risk/reward was 1:10.7 because EVERYTHING lined up. That's rare.

Replicating This Trade in Your Account

The Trading Checklist

Before you enter any swing trade:

  • [ ] Pattern: Clear technical pattern identified (H&S, cup & handle, etc.)
  • [ ] Support: Price tested support level and bounced
  • [ ] RSI: RSI below 40 or above 60, showing momentum
  • [ ] Volume: Volume increasing on bounce, not declining
  • [ ] Stop: Stop loss defined within 3-5% of entry
  • [ ] Catalyst: News, earnings, or event in next 2-4 weeks
  • [ ] Risk/Reward: Potential gain is at least 2x the potential loss
  • [ ] Sizing: Position size is 1-3% of account max
  • [ ] Targets: Profit targets defined before entry
  • [ ] Discipline: Promise to yourself you won't move the stop loss

If you check all 10 boxes, your trade has James's qualities.
