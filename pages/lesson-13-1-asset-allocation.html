Lesson 13.1: Strategic Asset Allocation

Overview

Asset allocation is the foundational strategy that determines your portfolio's risk and return profile. Rather than picking individual stocks, you're deciding what percentage of your capital goes to different asset classes.

Key Concepts

The Three Core Pillars

  1. Stocks (Equities)

- Growth potential

- Higher volatility

- Dividends and capital appreciation

  1. Bonds (Fixed Income)

- Stability and income

- Lower volatility

- Capital preservation

  1. Cash and Alternatives

- Liquidity

- Emergency reserves

- Diversification hedge

Allocation Frameworks

Classic Age-Based Model


Stock Allocation % = 100 - Your Age

or (Conservative) = 80 - Your Age



Examples:

- Age 25: 75% stocks, 25% bonds

- Age 40: 60% stocks, 40% bonds

- Age 55: 45% stocks, 55% bonds

- Age 70: 30% stocks, 70% bonds

Modern 3-Fund Portfolio


Conservative:    30% Stocks / 60% Bonds / 10% Alternatives

Moderate:        60% Stocks / 35% Bonds / 5% Alternatives

Aggressive:      85% Stocks / 10% Bonds / 5% Alternatives

Growth-focused:  90% Stocks / 5% Bonds / 5% Cash

Tactical Asset Allocation (TAA)

Adjust allocations based on:

  • Economic cycle stage
  • Interest rate environment
  • Market valuations
  • Sector strength
  • Momentum indicators

Example TAA Adjustment:

  • Bull market, low rates: Increase stocks to 80%
  • Rising rates environment: Increase bonds to 50%
  • Recession warning: Move to 40/40/20 defensive

Risk Tolerance Assessment

Portfolio Risk Profile


Your Allocation = f(Time Horizon, Risk Tolerance, Income Needs, Goals)

High Risk Tolerance:

  • Can handle 30-40% drawdowns
  • Long time horizon (10+ years)
  • Stable income source
  • No immediate capital needs

Medium Risk Tolerance:

  • Can handle 15-25% drawdowns
  • Medium time horizon (5-10 years)
  • Moderate income needs
  • Some upcoming capital needs

Low Risk Tolerance:

  • Can only handle 5-10% drawdowns
  • Short time horizon (under 5 years)
  • Income-dependent
  • Frequent capital withdrawals

## Implementation Methods



### Option 1: ETF Portfolio (Easiest)

**Advantages:**

- Low fees (0.03-0.20%)

- Instant diversification

- Easy rebalancing

- Tax-efficient



**Sample Portfolio:**

- 40% VOO (S&P 500)

- 20% VB (Small-cap)

- 20% VXUS (International)

- 15% BND (Total Bond)

- 5% Cash/Short-term



### Option 2: Individual Stocks (Higher Skill)

**Advantages:**

- No fund fees

- Direct ownership

- Tax-loss harvesting easier

- Customization



**Requirements:**

- Fundamental analysis skills

- Time commitment (10+ hours/week)

- Disciplined rebalancing

- Risk management



**Sample Allocation:**

- 50% Dividend stocks (10-15 holdings)

- 25% Growth stocks (5-8 holdings)

- 15% Bonds/Bond funds

- 10% Cash/Short-term assets



### Option 3: Blended Approach (Recommended)

60% ETFs (Core holdings)

- 30% Index funds (S&P 500, Total Market)

- 20% International/Bond ETFs

- 10% Sector/Alternative ETFs

40% Individual Stocks (Satellite)

- 20% Dividend stocks

- 15% Growth stocks

- 5% Speculative/Research

## Common Allocation Mistakes



### 1. Over-concentration

**Problem:** 80% in one sector or stock

**Solution:** Maintain maximum 15% in any single position



### 2. Emotional Allocation

**Problem:** Chasing recent winners

**Solution:** Use systematic, data-driven allocation



### 3. Neglecting Bonds

**Problem:** "I'm young, bonds are boring"

**Solution:** Bonds reduce drawdowns and provide rebalancing funds



### 4. Static Allocation

**Problem:** Never adjusting as life changes

**Solution:** Review and adjust annually or at major life events



### 5. Excessive Complexity

**Problem:** 50+ different holdings with no clear strategy

**Solution:** Keep it simple‚Äî20-30 holdings maximum



## Rebalancing Triggers



Set predetermined times to rebalance:

- **Calendar:** Annually or quarterly

- **Threshold:** When any allocation drifts >5% from target

- **Major Event:** Job change, inheritance, market crash



## Key Takeaways



1. **Asset allocation is the #1 determinant of risk/return** (90% of portfolio variance)

2. **Diversification reduces unsystematic risk** without sacrificing returns

3. **Your allocation should match your life stage and goals**

4. **Simple is better than complex** (3-5 core positions beat 50 mediocre ones)

5. **Stick with your allocation** through market cycles

6. **Rebalance systematically** to lock in gains and buy dips
