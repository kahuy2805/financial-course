Overview

Chart patterns are visual formations on price charts that repeat regularly and predict future price movement. In this lesson, you'll learn the most common and reliable patterns: Head and Shoulders, Double Tops, Double Bottoms, and Triangles. These patterns are used by professional traders worldwide because they work consistently.

Time to complete: 25-30 minutes

Prerequisites

  • Completed Lessons 4.1-4.5 (Charts through Volume)
  • Comfortable reading candlestick charts
  • Understand support, resistance, and volume
  • Access to a charting platform

Core Content

What Are Chart Patterns?

Chart patterns are distinctive shapes formed by price action that appear repeatedly and predict future movement.

Simple Analogy:

Think of weather patterns. Low pressure systems typically bring rain. You see the pattern on a weather map and predict rain. Chart patterns work similarly - when you see the pattern, you can predict the likely price move.

Why Chart Patterns Work

Reason 1: Trader Psychology

When traders see a pattern they recognize, they act the same way. This collective action creates predictable outcomes.

Reason 2: Historical Repetition

These patterns have repeated thousands of times over decades. Professionals trust them because they work.

Reason 3: Institutional Programs

Large funds have algorithms that recognize these patterns. Automated buying/selling at pattern completion creates reliable moves.

How to Identify Head and Shoulders

Step 1: Look for the Structure

  • Stock makes a peak (left shoulder)
  • Falls and bounces to a HIGHER peak (head)
  • Falls again and bounces to a LOWER peak (right shoulder)
  • Creates a valley at the bottom (neckline)

Step 2: Draw the Neckline

Draw a horizontal (or slightly sloped) line through the valleys.

Step 3: Watch for the Breakdown

When price breaks BELOW the neckline, the pattern is confirmed.

Pattern Reliability and Confirmation

What Makes Patterns More Reliable?

1. Higher Volume at Pattern Completion


Good: Triangle breaks out on 5x normal volume

Bad: Triangle breaks out on low volume



High volume = More conviction = More likely to work

2. Multiple Timeframe Confirmation


Good: Pattern appears on daily AND 4-hour chart

Bad: Pattern only on 1-hour chart



Larger timeframes = More reliable patterns

3. Larger Patterns


Good: Double Top formed over 2 months

Bad: Double Top formed over 2 days



Longer formation = More people aware of pattern = More reliable

Pattern Success Rates

Professional traders report these approximate success rates:

Head and Shoulders: 90% (very reliable)

Double Top/Bottom: 85% (very reliable)

Triangle: 80% (reliable)

These high success rates are why professionals trade patterns.

Real-World Application

Scenario 1: Identifying a Double Top

What You See:

Apple (AAPL) chart:

  • Rises to $150 (Peak 1)
  • Falls to $140
  • Rises to $150 again (Peak 2)
  • Support at $140 (neckline)

Your Analysis:

This looks like a double top formation.

Your Preparation:

  • Set SELL alert at $139 (below neckline)
  • Set stop loss at $152 (above peaks)
  • Set profit target at $130 ($140 neckline minus $10 peak distance)

When It Breaks:

Price breaks below $140 on high volume.

Your Action:

SELL 100 shares at $138-139

Stop loss: $152

Target: $130

Result: If target hit, you profit $8-9 per share.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake #1: Trading Incomplete Patterns

What Traders Do Wrong:

"I see a double top forming. I'll short it NOW before it completes."

Why It's Wrong:

Pattern isn't confirmed until price breaks the neckline. Shorting too early = higher risk.

The Right Way:

Wait for the breakdown BELOW neckline before entering.

Mistake #2: Ignoring Volume on Pattern Completion

Reality: A pattern that breaks on low volume often fails.

Always check: Did volume INCREASE on the breakout?

Mistake #3: Not Measuring the Pattern Correctly

What Traders Do Wrong:

"Double top is at $150. I'll set target at $130 (arbitrary choice)."

Why It's Wrong:

You're not using the pattern's geometry.

The Right Way:

Measure the peak-to-neckline distance and use that to set targets.

Mistake #4: Trading Patterns on Very Short Timeframes

Reality: Patterns on 1-minute charts are noise. Use at least 15-minute charts, preferably hourly or daily.

Mistake #5: Forcing Patterns That Don't Exist

What Traders Do Wrong:

"This could be a double top if I squint hard enough."

Reality: Real patterns are OBVIOUS. If you have to force it, it's not a valid pattern.

Quick Review

Chart Patterns:

Head and Shoulders (Reversal):

  • M-shape pattern (left shoulder, head, right shoulder)
  • Signals: Uptrend ending, downtrend coming
  • Trade: SELL on breakdown below neckline
  • Success rate: 90%

Double Top (Reversal):

  • M-shape, simpler than head/shoulders
  • Signals: Resistance tested twice, breakdown coming
  • Trade: SELL on breakdown below neckline
  • Success rate: 85%

Double Bottom (Reversal):

  • W-shape pattern
  • Signals: Support tested twice, breakout coming
  • Trade: BUY on breakout above neckline
  • Success rate: 85%

Triangle (Continuation):

  • Converging lines, narrowing range
  • Signals: Pressure building, breakout coming (usually up)
  • Trade: BUY/SELL on breakout
  • Success rate: 80%

Key Principles:

  1. Patterns must be clearly visible (not forced)
  2. Volume should INCREASE on pattern completion
  3. Larger patterns (longer formation) = More reliable
  4. Use multiple timeframes for confirmation
  5. Always use stop losses

Practice Exercise

Exercise 1: Pattern Identification

Look at a daily chart of 5 different stocks.

For each stock, find:

  1. Is there a chart pattern visible? (Which one?)
  2. Is it complete or still forming?
  3. If complete, did volume confirm it?
  4. Would you trade this pattern? (Why/why not)

Exercise 2: Double Top Setup

Find a stock with a completed double top pattern (price broke neckline).

  1. Where were the two peaks?
  2. What was the neckline level?
  3. What was the breakdown (how far did price fall)?
  4. What would have been your profit target?
  5. Did the target get hit?

Exercise 3: Triangle Prediction

Find a stock forming a triangle.

  1. When did the triangle start?
  2. What's the height of the triangle?
  3. Where are the converging support/resistance lines?
  4. When will it break (guess)?
  5. Which direction do you think it will break?

Exercise 4: Multi-Timeframe Pattern Confirmation

Find a pattern on a daily chart.

Now check:

  1. Is the same pattern visible on 4-hour chart? (YES/NO)
  2. Is it visible on 1-hour chart? (YES/NO)
  3. How confident are you in this pattern? (Rate 1-10)
  4. Would you increase your position size if multiple timeframes confirm?

Exercise 5: Pattern History Research

Pick a stock that showed a chart pattern in the past.

Research:

  1. When did the pattern form?
  2. What did technical traders predict would happen?
  3. What actually happened?
  4. Did the pattern work or fail?
  5. What was the percentage move?
