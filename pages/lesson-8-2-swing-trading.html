Lesson 8.2: Swing Trading Fundamentals

Learning Objectives

  • Understand multi-day position management (3-14 day holds)
  • Identify swing trade setups on daily and 4-hour charts
  • Apply swing trading indicators effectively
  • Manage swing trades across multiple holdings

What is Swing Trading?

Swing trading captures 3-14 day price moves using daily and 4-hour timeframes. It balances the frequency of day trading with the holding periods of position trading.

Key Characteristics

  • Holding Period: 3-14 days typically
  • Trading Timeframe: 4-hour and daily charts
  • Capital Requirement: $5,000 minimum (no PDT rules apply)
  • Daily Time Commitment: 30-60 minutes per day
  • Typical Gain per Trade: 2%-8%
  • Risk per Trade: 1%-2% of account

Swing Trading Setup Examples

Setup 1: Pullback to Moving Average

The Strategy:

  • Stock in uptrend (price above 50-day MA)
  • Price pulls back to 50-day MA
  • Enter on bounce from MA
  • Stop below MA and recent swing low

Example with $15,000 Portfolio:

Stock: MSFT
- 50-day MA: $350.00
- Recent Swing Low: $348.50
- Current Price: $345.00 (pullback)
- Support Confluence: 50-day MA + swing low

Entry Setup:
- Buy 35 shares at $350.10 (above MA)
- Stop Loss: $348.00 (-2.10 per share = -$73.50)
- Target 1: $358.00 (+8.00 per share)
- Target 2: $365.00 (+15.00 per share)

Risk: $73.50 (0.49%)
Position Size: 35 shares
Time Horizon: 5-10 days

Setup 2: Double Bottom Reversal

The Strategy:

  • Downtrend established
  • Price tests support level twice
  • Second test holds above first test
  • Reversal expected on break above resistance

Example with $20,000 Portfolio:

Stock: AMD
- First Support Test: $95.00
- Second Support Test: $95.50 (held higher)
- Resistance (neckline): $102.00

Setup:
- Buy 150 shares at $102.50 (above resistance)
- Stop Loss: $94.80 (-7.70 per share = -$1,155)

Position Size Adjustment:
- Reduce risk to 1% = $200 max
- Position size: $200 / $7.70 = 26 shares

Risk: $200 (1%)
Reward Target: $110.00 (+7.50 = +$195)
Risk-Reward: 1:0.98 (Adjust targets)

BETTER Setup:
- Target: $115.00 (+12.50 = +$325)
- Risk-Reward: 1:1.63
- Hold: 7-12 days expected

The Strategy:

Swing Trading with 4-Hour Charts

Why 4-Hour Charts?

  • Filters out intraday noise
  • Captures swings within the daily trend
  • Better entry precision than daily charts
  • Identifies optimal entry within daily range

4-Hour Chart Setup Example

Daily Trend: UPTREND (price > 20-day MA)
4-Hour Setup: Consolidation breakout

Daily High: $242.50
Daily Low: $238.00

Within the day, 4-hour consolidation forms:
- High: $241.00
- Low: $239.50

Swing Trade:
- Entry: $241.10 (breakout above 4-hour high)
- Stop: $239.00 (below 4-hour consolidation)
- Target: Previous 4-hour high + range extension
- Hold: 2-8 hours, extend if still in trend

Indicator Selection for Swing Trading

Key Indicators

#### 1. Moving Averages (20-day, 50-day, 200-day)

Usage:
- 20-day MA: Short-term trend confirmation
- 50-day MA: Entry pullback level
- 200-day MA: Long-term trend direction

Example:
Price > 50-day MA > 200-day MA = UPTREND confirmed
Can buy pullbacks to 50-day MA

#### 2. RSI (Relative Strength Index)

Settings: 14-period
- RSI > 70: Overbought (potential pullback)
- RSI < 30: Oversold (potential bounce)
- Divergence: Price high but RSI lower = reversal signal

Swing Trading Usage:
- Enter long when RSI bounces off 30 (oversold)
- Exit when RSI approaches 70 (overbought)
- Look for divergence for trend reversals

#### 3. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Components:
- MACD line (fast)
- Signal line (slow)
- Histogram (difference)

Swing Trading Signals:
- BUY: MACD crosses above signal line
- SELL: MACD crosses below signal line
- Divergence: MACD strengthening but price weakening = reversal

Example:
Stock in uptrend
MACD crosses above signal line ‚Üí BUY signal
Hold through higher lows
Exit on MACD cross below signal line or divergence

#### 4. Bollinger Bands

Usage:
- Price near upper band: Potential resistance/exit
- Price near lower band: Potential support/entry
- Band squeeze: Low volatility, breakout coming
- Band expansion: High volatility, trend acceleration

Swing Trade Example:
- Price bounces at lower band ‚Üí BUY
- Set stop below lower band
- Target: Upper band or previous resistance

Common Swing Trading Mistakes

  1. Too large position sizes: Risk explodes if stop hit
  2. Ignoring daily chart: Entering against main trend
  3. Holding through earnings: Overnight gap risk in swing position
  4. Moving stop loss down: Removes exit discipline
  5. Holding winners too long: Giving back profits on reversal
  6. Not scaling into positions: Missing position on dips
  7. Over-trading: Taking marginal setups instead of high-quality ones

Swing Trading Timeframe Rules

  • Minimum hold: 2-3 days (let trade develop)
  • Maximum hold: 12-14 days (swing deteriorates)
  • Optimal hold: 5-8 days (best reward/risk window)
  • Weekly rebalance: Check positions every Friday

Summary

Swing trading bridges day trading and position trading:

  • Holds positions 3-14 days for 2%-8% gains
  • Uses daily and 4-hour charts for setups
  • Employs moving average pullbacks and chart patterns
  • Manages 3-4 positions simultaneously
  • Risk of 1%-1.5% per position for portfolio diversification

Key to success: Patience for setup quality and discipline on exits.

Key Takeaways

  1. Higher lows with higher highs identify uptrend swings
  2. Pullbacks to 50-day MA provide high-probability entries
  3. Use 4-hour charts for entry precision within daily trend
  4. Scale positions to 1.5% risk each for multiple holdings
  5. Partial profit-taking at 1:1 locks in gains
  6. Hold remaining position for 2:1+ targets
  7. Exit on MACD/RSI divergence or time/target hit
