Simulation Scenario 01: Your First $10K Portfolio

Overview

You have $10,000 to invest. You're going to build a portfolio from scratch over 12 weeks. This scenario teaches fundamental portfolio building: position sizing, diversification, discipline, and profit-taking.

Difficulty: Beginner

Duration: 12 weeks (April - June 2024 hypothetical)

Starting Capital: $10,000

Goal: Learn to invest without major losses, target 5-12% gain

Market Context: Stable market, no major crashes

Your Starting Position

Account Setup

  • Cash: $10,000
  • Stocks owned: None
  • Margin: Not available (keeping it simple)
  • Risk tolerance: You can handle 5-10% portfolio decline without panic

Your Rules (Before You Start)

Write these down NOW before you make any trades:


Rule 1: No single stock > 20% of portfolio

Rule 2: No position without a stop loss

Rule 3: Max 5 positions at any time

Rule 4: Sell 50% when up 30%, hold rest

Rule 5: Sell 100% when down 15% (stop loss)

Rule 6: Check portfolio weekly, not daily

Rule 7: No borrowing or margin

Rule 8: Cash minimum 10% of portfolio

Do you agree with these rules? If not, write your own.

Lessons from Your First Portfolio

Lesson 1: Discipline Beats Emotion

  • You had a plan at the start (buy SPY, MSFT, KO)
  • You stuck to it even when markets dipped
  • Result: Small but consistent gain

If you had panic-sold on June 3, you'd be down 3.8%. Instead you're up 6.86%.

Lesson 2: Diversification Protects You

Your 4 positions allowed you to:

  • Get broad market (SPY)
  • Pick winners (MSFT)
  • Get income (KO)
  • Add diversity (Healthcare)

If you'd put all $10,000 in MSFT, your return would be +8.2% (better!). But if MSFT had crashed, you'd lose more too.

Diversification = safety + consistent returns.

Lesson 3: Cash is a Position

Your $1,000 cash reserve:

  • Let you sleep at night
  • Prevented forced selling
  • Would have let you buy a crash (if one happened)
  • Is boring but valuable

Many new investors get this wrong. Cash feels "wasteful" but it's actually strategic.

Lesson 4: Your Rules Worked

You wrote down 8 rules at the start. Did they help?

  • Rule 1 (20% max per position): Kept you from overleveraging
  • Rule 2 (Stop loss): Gave you an exit plan (never triggered)
  • Rule 3 (5 positions max): Kept it simple
  • Rule 4 (Sell 50% at +30%): Didn't trigger, but would have locked profits
  • Rule 5 (Sell at -15%): Provided safety net (never triggered)
  • Rule 6 (Weekly not daily): Kept you from overtrading
  • Rule 7 (No margin): Kept you safe
  • Rule 8 (10% cash): Gave you flexibility

Lesson: Having rules and following them beats trying to time the market.

Lesson 5: Time and Patience

12 weeks is 3 months. Your 6.86% return is annualized to:

  • 6.86% √ó 4 = ~27% annualized

That's excellent. But you made it by:

  • Being patient
  • Not trading much (only initial entry)
  • Holding through volatility
  • Following your plan

This is how wealth is built. Not exciting, but reliable.

If You Lost Money (Down 5%+)

- Did you panic sell?

  • Try again but with slightly more aggressive positions
  • Or try scenario 01 again during a different market period
