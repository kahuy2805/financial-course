## ‚úçÔ∏è WRITING STANDARDS & STYLE GUIDE

### Beginner-Friendly Writing Principles

1. **Clarity First**
   - Use simple, concrete language
   - Define all jargon on first use
   - Prefer active voice over passive
   - Maximum sentence length: 25 words

2. **Progressive Complexity**
   - Introduce one new concept at a time
   - Build on previously learned material
   - Use scaffolding techniques
   - Reference earlier lessons when needed

3. **Engagement Techniques**
   - Start with relatable real-world examples
   - Use analogies from everyday life
   - Include visual descriptions where helpful
   - Add "Think About It" reflection prompts

4. **Structure per Lesson**
   ```markdown
   # Lesson Title

   ## Overview (2-3 sentences)
   What you'll learn and why it matters

   ## Prerequisites
   What you should know before starting

   ## Core Content (800-1200 words)
   - Key Concept 1 (explanation + example)
   - Key Concept 2 (explanation + example)
   - Key Concept 3 (explanation + example)

   ## Real-World Application
   How this applies to actual trading

   ## Common Mistakes to Avoid
   Pitfalls beginners often encounter

   ## Quick Review
   - Bullet point summary of key takeaways

   ## Practice Exercise
   Hands-on activity to reinforce learning

   ## Next Steps
   What lesson comes next and why
   ```

5. **Tone & Voice**
   - Encouraging and supportive
   - Professional yet approachable
   - Patient and non-judgmental
   - Realistic about challenges

## üîç QUALITY CRITERIA CHECKLIST

Before considering any lesson complete, verify:

### Content Quality
- [ ] Factually accurate with current market practices
- [ ] No misleading information or unrealistic expectations
- [ ] Balanced perspective (acknowledges risks and rewards)
- [ ] Citations for statistics or specific claims

### Pedagogical Effectiveness
- [ ] Clear learning objective stated upfront
- [ ] Logical flow from simple to complex
- [ ] Sufficient examples and illustrations
- [ ] Practice opportunity provided
- [ ] Success criteria defined

### Accessibility
- [ ] Readable at 8th-grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid)
- [ ] All technical terms defined
- [ ] No unexplained acronyms
- [ ] Formatting aids readability (headings, lists, emphasis)

### Practical Relevance
- [ ] Connects theory to real trading scenarios
- [ ] Includes actionable steps
- [ ] Addresses common beginner questions
- [ ] Provides risk warnings where appropriate

## üí° INTENT INTERPRETATION PROMPTS

When a user provides a request, ask clarifying questions:

### Understanding the Learner
- What is the learner's current knowledge level?
- Do they have any prior investing/trading experience?
- What is their primary goal (career, personal wealth, side income)?
- How much time can they dedicate to learning?

### Defining Scope
- Should we focus on specific markets (US stocks, forex, options)?
- Day trading, swing trading, or long-term investing focus?
- What depth of technical/fundamental analysis is needed?
- Should we include specific platforms or tools?

### Customization Preferences
- Any specific teaching methods preferred?
- Need for visual aids, videos, or purely text?
- Assessment style (quizzes, projects, simulations)?
- Mentorship format (Q&A, regular check-ins, case reviews)?

### Constraints & Boundaries
- Any topics to avoid or emphasize?
- Regulatory considerations for target audience?
- Time constraints for course completion?
- Budget considerations for trading capital examples?
