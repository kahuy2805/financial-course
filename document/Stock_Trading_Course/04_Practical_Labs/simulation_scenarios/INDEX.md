# Simulation Scenarios - Interactive Trading Challenges

## Overview

Simulation scenarios are interactive trading challenges where you make real-time decisions with real market data (historical). Each scenario presents a starting situation, market conditions, and decision points. You must make buy/sell/hold decisions and learn from outcomes.

These are NOT backtests. They're educational simulations designed to test your decision-making, risk management, and psychology under realistic conditions.

---

## 10 Simulation Scenarios

### Scenario 01: Your First $10K Portfolio (Beginner)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Duration**: 12 weeks
- **Starting Capital**: $10,000
- **Goal**: Don't lose more than 5%, learn position sizing
- **Market Context**: Stable market conditions, no crashes
- **Focus**: Basic decision-making, discipline, avoiding common mistakes

**Key Decision Points**:
1. How much per position? (Avoid concentration)
2. When to take profits? (Avoid greed)
3. When to cut losses? (Avoid stubbornness)
4. How often to trade? (Avoid overtrading)

**Possible Outcomes**: +3% to +12% or -5% to +5%

### Scenario 02: Managing Through Volatility (Intermediate)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Duration**: 8 weeks
- **Starting Capital**: $25,000
- **Goal**: Maintain portfolio through 30% market decline
- **Market Context**: Fed rate hikes, inflation concerns, market volatility increases
- **Focus**: Psychology, portfolio diversification, staying invested

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Do you add to positions in the decline? (DCA vs panic)
2. Do you shift to defensive holdings? (Rebalancing)
3. Do you panic sell at the bottom? (Discipline test)
4. Do you get FOMO when market bounces? (Greed test)

**Possible Outcomes**: -25% to +15% depending on psychology

### Scenario 03: Earnings Season Challenge (Intermediate)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Duration**: 6 weeks (Earnings season)
- **Starting Capital**: $30,000
- **Goal**: Profit from earnings moves while managing binary risk
- **Market Context**: Multiple stocks reporting earnings daily
- **Focus**: IV crush, position sizing, catalyst trading

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Which stocks to trade earnings? (Selection)
2. How to structure? (Stock vs calls vs spreads)
3. When to exit? (Before or after?)
4. How to size? (Defined risk)

**Possible Outcomes**: +15% to -20% depending on earnings calls

### Scenario 04: Recovery from 50% Drawdown (Advanced)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Duration**: 6 months
- **Starting Capital**: $100,000 (drops to $50,000 quickly)
- **Goal**: Recover portfolio to breakeven and beyond
- **Market Context**: Your choices caused the loss (concentration + leverage), now rebuild
- **Focus**: Psychology, discipline, systematic recovery

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Do you panic sell at the bottom? (Discipline)
2. Can you implement a recovery plan? (Discipline)
3. Can you stick to DCA for 6 months? (Patience)
4. Can you avoid over-trading? (Control)

**Possible Outcomes**: -50% (if you continue bad habits) to +20% (if disciplined)

### Scenario 05: Sector Rotation Timing (Intermediate)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Duration**: 6 months
- **Starting Capital**: $50,000
- **Goal**: Beat the S&P 500 by rotating sectors
- **Market Context**: Economic cycle with sector leadership rotation
- **Focus**: Macro awareness, sector momentum, exit discipline

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Which sector to buy? (Analysis)
2. When to rotate? (Timing)
3. How to exit? (Profit-taking)
4. Do you hold too long? (Greed)

**Possible Outcomes**: -10% to +35% depending on rotation timing

### Scenario 06: Solo Growth Stock Trade (Beginner to Intermediate)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner to Intermediate
- **Duration**: 4-8 weeks
- **Starting Capital**: $10,000
- **Goal**: Successfully swing trade a growth stock
- **Market Context**: One specific growth stock with technical setup
- **Focus**: Technical analysis, position sizing, risk management

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Do you identify the pattern? (Analysis)
2. Do you set a stop loss? (Risk management)
3. Do you scale out of profits? (Discipline)
4. Do you hold too long? (Greed)

**Possible Outcomes**: -10% to +40% depending on execution

### Scenario 07: Options Income Strategy (Intermediate to Advanced)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced
- **Duration**: 3 months (Multiple option cycles)
- **Starting Capital**: $50,000
- **Goal**: Generate consistent 2-3% monthly income
- **Market Context**: Stable market, defined income opportunities
- **Focus**: Options mechanics, risk definition, profit consistency

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Which options strategies? (Covered calls, cash-secured puts, spreads)
2. How much margin? (Leverage decision)
3. When to close? (Discipline)
4. Can you avoid blow-ups? (Risk management)

**Possible Outcomes**: +4% to +12% monthly or blow-up to -30%+

### Scenario 08: IPO and First Public Offering (Intermediate)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Duration**: 3 months post-IPO
- **Starting Capital**: $15,000
- **Goal**: Profit from IPO pop and post-IPO consolidation
- **Market Context**: A company just went public, high volatility expected
- **Focus**: IPO mechanics, volatility management, momentum

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Do you buy the IPO pop? (Risky, exciting)
2. Do you wait for a pullback? (Patient, safer)
3. When do you exit? (Quick profit vs hold)
4. Do you chase? (FOMO risk)

**Possible Outcomes**: -25% (if pop bought then crashes) to +40% (if pullback timed right)

### Scenario 09: Defending Against Black Swan Event (Advanced)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Duration**: 2 weeks (Crisis)
- **Starting Capital**: $100,000
- **Goal**: Protect portfolio from sudden 20%+ crash
- **Market Context**: Unexpected news (geopolitical, natural disaster, economic shock)
- **Focus**: Crisis management, hedging, psychology under pressure

**Key Decision Points**:
1. Do you panic sell? (Emotional risk)
2. Do you buy the dip? (Opportunity vs risk)
3. Do you hedge? (If so, how?)
4. Do you do nothing? (Often the right answer)

**Possible Outcomes**: -25% to -5% depending on actions taken

### Scenario 10: Year-Long Portfolio Building (Advanced)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Duration**: 12 months
- **Starting Capital**: $25,000 + $500/month additions
- **Goal**: Build portfolio to $35,000+ while learning
- **Market Context**: Full year with all market conditions (growth, decline, volatility, stability)
- **Focus**: Everything - position sizing, diversification, psychology, discipline

**Key Decision Points**: Dozens of decisions across 12 months (this is the ultimate test)

**Possible Outcomes**: +20% to -30% depending on decision quality

## How to Use These Simulations

### Option 1: Self-Directed Simulation
1. **Pick a scenario** that matches your level
2. **Use real historical data** (or play along with today's market)
3. **Make decisions** - would you buy here? Sell here? How much?
4. **Track outcomes** - what happened to your decisions?
5. **Analyze results** - what did you do right/wrong?

### Option 2: Instructor-Led Simulation
1. **Join online group** (if available)
2. **Make decisions as group** (democratic or by role)
3. **Get real-time feedback**
4. **Discuss psychology** (why you made decisions)
5. **Learn from others' mistakes**

### Option 3: Time-Shifted Simulation
1. **Pick historical period** (e.g., March 2020 crash)
2. **Pretend it's that date** (don't look at outcomes)
3. **Make decisions blind** (no hindsight)
4. **Then see what actually happened**
5. **Analyze your decisions vs reality**

## The Psychology Factor

Each simulation is designed to test your psychology:

1. **Greeding you out**: Will you hold winners too long?
2. **Scaring you out**: Will you panic sell lows?
3. **Tempting you**: Will you overtrade?
4. **Lulling you**: Will you get overconfident?
5. **Pressuring you**: Will you follow the crowd?

The traders who succeed in these simulations:
- Are disciplined, not lucky
- Stick to plans, don't improvise
- Accept losses, don't revenge-trade
- Take profits, don't chase
- Stay calm, don't panic

## Common Mistakes in Simulations

### Mistake 1: Not Following Your Plan
- You say "I'll exit at $50"
- Stock at $49, you wait
- Stock at $52, you panic sell
- **Result**: Sell at $52 instead of $50 (worse price)

### Mistake 2: Overtrading
- Scenario 01: Supposed to buy 3-4 times in 12 weeks
- You buy/sell 25 times
- **Result**: Transaction costs kill returns, psychological exhaustion

### Mistake 3: Overleveraging
- You have $10,000 capital
- You buy $15,000 in stocks with margin
- Market drops 20%
- You're forced to sell at worst prices
- **Result**: Turn -20% market loss into -50% personal loss

### Mistake 4: Panic in Downturns
- Market down 10% (expected in normal years)
- You sell everything
- Market recovers to new highs in 6 weeks
- **Result**: Missed recovery, locked in losses

### Mistake 5: Greed in Uptrends
- Stock up 30% (your profit target)
- You think "Maybe it goes to 50%"
- Stock falls 20% from peak
- You exit at -10% (negative for month)
- **Result**: Didn't take profits, turned win into loss

## Real Money vs Simulation

**Simulations teach you:**
- Decision-making process
- Risk management fundamentals
- Emotional management
- Pattern recognition
- Probability thinking

**Simulations DON'T teach you:**
- Real money psychology (stakes are different)
- Opportunity cost (missing other trades)
- Real execution difficulty
- Real slippage and fees
- Real volatility impact

**Therefore:**
- Do simulations first
- Then try real money with very small amounts ($100-$500)
- Scale up as you prove consistency
- Never risk more than you can afford to lose

## Learning Loops

Each simulation should follow this cycle:

```
Decision → Outcome → Analysis → Lesson → New Rule → Next Scenario
```

Example:
- **Decision**: Hold stock because it "might go higher"
- **Outcome**: Stock down 15% from peak
- **Analysis**: I was greedy, didn't follow my profit target
- **Lesson**: Profits locked in are profits earned
- **New Rule**: Exit at predetermined target, no exceptions
- **Next scenario**: Use the new rule and track if it works better
