# Simulation Scenario 02: Managing Through Volatility

## Overview

Your $25,000 portfolio must survive a 30% market decline while you manage your emotions, rebalance, and make smart capital deployment decisions. This scenario teaches psychology, diversification, and discipline during bear market conditions.

**Difficulty**: Intermediate
**Duration**: 8 weeks
**Starting Capital**: $25,000
**Goal**: Lose less than 20%, ideally stay above -15%
**Market Context**: Fed rate hike shock, 30% S&P 500 decline expected

---

## The Setup

### Your Starting Portfolio

You arrive at the simulation with this existing allocation:

```
$12,000  S&P 500 ETF (SPY)        - 48%
$5,000   Tech ETF (QQQ)           - 20%
$3,000   Bond ETF (BND)           - 12%
$2,000   International (VXUS)     - 8%
$2,000   Dividend Aristocrat (SDY)- 8%
$1,000   Cash                     - 4%
─────────────────────────────────────
$25,000  Total Portfolio          - 100%
```

### Your Rules Going In

Before the scenario starts, you have these principles:

1. **Hold core positions** - Don't panic sell index funds
2. **Rebalance when out of balance** - Sell high, buy low
3. **Don't use margin** - Keep capital safe
4. **Buy dips strategically** - If available capital, consider adding
5. **Psychological check-ins** - Weekly reflection
6. **Stay invested** - Market downturns are temporary

---

## Week 1: The Shock (The Trigger Event)

### The Catalyst

**Wednesday, June 15, 2024 - 2:00 PM**

Federal Reserve surprises market:
- Inflation hotter than expected: CPI at 4.2% (up from 3.5%)
- Fed signals "higher for longer" policy
- Rate hikes might not be done despite pause
- Market expects rates to stay at 5.5% for 2025

### Immediate Market Reaction

```
Wed 2:00 PM: Announcement made
Wed 2:05 PM: SPY at $530 → $510 (-3.8% in 5 minutes)
Wed 3:00 PM: SPY at $510 → $500 (-5.7% for day)
Wed 4:00 PM: SPY closes at $502 (-5.3% close)

Your portfolio immediately down:
SPY:     $12,000 → $11,288 (-$712)
QQQ:     $5,000 → $4,650 (-$350) [tech worse hit]
BND:     $3,000 → $2,940 (-$60) [bonds down too]
VXUS:    $2,000 → $1,900 (-$100)
SDY:     $2,000 → $1,940 (-$60)
Cash:    $1,000 → $1,000 (unchanged)

Portfolio: $25,000 → $23,718 (-$1,282) -5.1% in ONE DAY
```

### Your Emotional State (Hour 1)

You see the decline unfolding. What goes through your mind?

**Thought 1**: "Oh no, this is bad. Should I sell everything?"
**Thought 2**: "Maybe I should wait and see what happens tomorrow"
**Thought 3**: "This is a panic sell-off, I should HOLD"
**Thought 4**: "Maybe I should buy the dip?"

**Your decision: What do you do right now?** _______________

**Recommended**: Option 3 - Hold and observe. One bad day is not a crisis.

### End of Week 1

```
Monday:   SPY $502 → $495 (-6.8%)
Tuesday:  SPY $495 → $510 (+3.0%, false bounce)
Wednesday: SPY $510 → $485 (-4.9%)
Thursday: SPY $485 → $488 (-0.6%)
Friday:   SPY $488 → $475 (-2.7%)

Friday close: Your portfolio
SPY:     $12,000 × (475/530) = $10,716 (-$1,284)
QQQ:     $5,000 × (drop of 10%) = $4,500 (-$500)
BND:     $3,000 × (slight gain) = $3,020 (+$20)
VXUS:    $2,000 × (down 6%) = $1,880 (-$120)
SDY:     $2,000 × (down 5%) = $1,900 (-$100)
Cash:    $1,000 ($1,000)

Portfolio value: $23,016 (-$1,984 total) -7.9% week 1
```

### Your Decision: Hold or Rebalance?

At this point, your allocations have shifted:

**Original allocation:**
- SPY 48%, QQQ 20%, BND 12%, VXUS 8%, SDY 8%, Cash 4%

**New allocation after decline:**
- SPY 46.5%, QQQ 19.6%, BND 13.1%, VXUS 8.2%, SDY 8.3%, Cash 4.3%

Not much change yet, but equities are lighter (from 76% to 73%) and bonds/cash heavier.

**Do you:**
- Option A: Rebalance back to original (means selling bonds, buying stocks) - Contrarian
- Option B: Hold current allocation (wait and see) - Cautious
- Option C: Reduce equity exposure (move to 50/50 stocks/bonds) - Defensive

**What's your choice?** _______________

**Recommended**: Option B - Hold and observe. Week 1 is too early to rebalance.

---

## Week 2-3: Capitulation Begins (The Panic Phase)

### Market Deterioration

Bad news compounds:

```
Monday: S&P 500 opens down 3% on global concerns
Tuesday: Down 4% more on recession fears
Wed-Thu: Down 2% more on corporate earnings warnings
Friday: Down 2% more on job market weakening

By Friday close: S&P 500 down 25% from peak (real bear market)
```

### Your Portfolio Week 2-3

```
Week 2 start: $23,016
SPY:  475 → 400 (-15.8% from original entry)
QQQ:  Falling with 20% drop from original
BND:  $3,020 → $3,100 (slight bond bounce, bonds up when stocks crash)
VXUS: $1,880 → $1,650 (down 12%)
SDY:  $1,900 → $1,700 (dividend stocks vulnerable)
Cash: $1,000

Week 2 end estimate: ~$19,500 (total down 22%)

By week 3: Things get worse as news gets worse

Week 3: More earnings warnings
SPY: 400 → $370 (-30% from original)
QQQ: Continues weak (tech hardest hit)
Market hits maximum pain: S&P 500 down 30% peak-to-trough

Estimated portfolio: $18,500 (total down 26%)
```

### Your Psychology at Maximum Pain

You're now down $6,500 (-26%) from your starting position.

**Common emotions you'll feel:**
1. **Fear**: "Will it go to zero?" (It won't, but that's the feeling)
2. **Regret**: "Should have sold in June" (Irrelevant now)
3. **Panic**: "I need to do something NOW" (Most dangerous)
4. **Resignation**: "I'm never getting this back" (Untrue, but feels real)

### Critical Decision Point 1: The Rebalancing Test

Your current allocation:
```
SPY:     ~50% of portfolio (still big)
QQQ:     ~18% (down from 20%)
BND:     ~17% (up from 12%) - Bonds gained when stocks crashed
VXUS:    ~9% (down from 8%)
SDY:     ~9% (down from 8%)
Cash:    ~5% (up from 4%)
```

**Your portfolio accidentally became more defensive** (bonds up to 17%, cash up to 5%).

**Rebalancing question**: Would you:

**Option A: Stay as is** - You've naturally become more defensive, which is good
**Option B: Buy stocks with bonds** - Bonds up, buy stocks down (contrarian)
**Option C: Move more to cash** - Get to 25% cash, reduce risk further (fearful)
**Option D: Buy stocks with cash** - Double down on pain (aggressive)

**What do you do?** _______________

**Recommended**: Option B (Slight) - Take 25% of bond gains, buy some dip
```
Action: Sell $500 of bonds
Buy: $500 of SPY at $370
New SPY position: $11,288 + $500 = $11,788
Net effect: Small dollar-cost average down
```

### Critical Decision Point 2: Your Stop Loss

You didn't set a stop loss at the start because "I'm investing long-term."

But now down 26%, questions emerge:
- Should I exit? (Cut the loss)
- Can I afford to go down more? (Psychologically)
- Is there a technical bottom? (No, unknowable)
- Will I panic sell at the worst time? (Possible)

**Do you:**
- Option A: Set a stop loss now at -30% (cuts losses)
- Option B: Set a stop loss at -40% (lets it run more)
- Option C: Don't set a stop loss (will hold come what may)
- Option D: Sell everything today (lock in loss)

**What's your choice?** _______________

**Recommended**: Option C - Don't set a stop loss. You have 5+ year horizon, this is temporary.

**BUT also**: Make a psychological commitment that you will NOT panic sell, no matter what.

---

## Week 4: The Bottom (Maximum Opportunity)

### The Inflection Point

News finally starts shifting after terrible week 3:

```
Monday: Fed signals more patience (won't hike again)
Tuesday: Market bounces +3%
Wednesday: Market adds +1.5% more
Thursday: Market adds +2%
Friday: Market adds +0.5%

Week total: S&P 500 up 7% from worst point
```

### The Psychology Flip

This is the critical moment.

**Week 3 you felt**:  Maximum pain, despair, fear
**Week 4 you feel**: Relief, hope, potential

This is also where most people make mistakes:
- They sold at the bottom (week 3) thinking it would go lower
- They're now sitting in cash, watching the bounce
- They tell themselves "I'll wait for another dip to buy back"
- But the bounce often becomes the start of recovery

### Your Decision: Did You Sell?

**Scenario A: You Panic Sold in Week 3**
```
At low: Portfolio worth $18,500
Sold everything in panic: Now holding $18,500 cash
Monday bounce: SPY goes from $370 → $381 (+3%)
Your portfolio: Still $18,500 cash (missed +3%)

You'll now feel FOMO ("I should buy back")
And if you do buy now, you bought higher than the bottom
And you have trading losses to show for it
```

**Scenario B: You Held (Recommended)**
```
Week 3 low: Portfolio $18,500
Week 4 bounce: SPY $370 → $381 (+3%)
Your portfolio: $18,500 × 1.03 = $19,055 (recovered $555 in one week!)
You feel relief and vindication (you held!)
You still down 24%, but optimism returns
```

**Did you sell or hold?** _______________

**If you sold**: No problem, but note that week 4 started recovery
**If you held**: Excellent, you learned the hardest lesson (selling at lows kills wealth)

### Market Continues Bouncing (Week 4 end)

```
Week 4 end:
SPY: $370 → $395 (+6.8% week, +7% from crash low)
Portfolio estimated: ~$19,700 (up to -21% from start)

You're starting to feel better, but still down significantly
```

---

## Week 5-6: The Recovery Rally (Hope Returns)

### Market Continues Up

```
Week 5:
SPY: $395 → $425 (+7.6%)
Portfolio: $19,700 → $21,300 (gain of $1,600 in one week)

Week 6:
SPY: $425 → $445 (+4.7%)
Portfolio: $21,300 → $22,200 (up another $900)
```

### Rebalancing Decision 2

Your allocations have shifted again:

```
Original: SPY 48%, QQQ 20%, BND 12%, VXUS 8%, SDY 8%, Cash 4%
Current: SPY 47%, QQQ 19%, BND 12%, VXUS 8%, SDY 8%, Cash 6%
```

Relatively balanced actually, because:
1. Bonds held up well (counterbalanced stock losses)
2. SPY recovered some
3. You still have 6% cash

**Do you:**
- Option A: Rebalance back to 48% SPY / 12% BND mix
- Option B: Move cash to stocks while still low
- Option C: Hold current allocation
- Option D: Move more to bonds (risk-off posture)

**What do you do?** _______________

**Recommended**: Option B - Use $100-200 of cash to average into stocks slightly

## Week 7-8: New Highs and Normalcy Returns

### Recovery Continues

```
Week 7:
SPY: $445 → $475 (+6.7%)
Fed signals rate stability, positive earnings surprise
Portfolio: $22,200 → $23,600 (recovered $1,400)

Week 8:
SPY: $475 → $500 (+5.3%)
Back near original entry level!
Portfolio: $23,600 → $24,800 (nearly full recovery)

Total portfolio: $24,800 of original $25,000 (-0.8%)
```

### End of Scenario

```
Starting: $25,000
Lowest point: $18,500 (-26% in week 3-4)
Ending: $24,800 (-0.8% total for 8 weeks)

Your journey:
Week 1: -7.9%
Week 3: -26% (worst)
Week 5: -15% (recovering)
Week 8: -0.8% (nearly breakeven)
```

**Decision 1: Hold in week 1** - Correct

- Holding let you recover

**Decision 2: Slight rebalancing into dip** - Correct

- Small dollar-cost average worked

**Decision 3: Hold through week 3 bottom** - Most important

- Psychology-based hold paid off

### What You Learned

1. **Bear markets are survivable** - 26% drawdown didn't wipe you out
2. **Diversification saved you** - Bonds up 7% while stocks down 30%

4. **Holding beats selling** - Every action would have been worse

## If You Didn't Hold

**If you panic sold in week 3 at -26%:**
- You locked in your loss
- You're sitting with $18,500 cash
- You watched week 4-8 recovery happening
- You tell yourself "I'll buy back on the next dip"
- But the next dip might not come for months
- Or you buy back at $475, which is higher than $370
- Result: -26% loss + missed recovery = -31% total damage

**This is why 95% of active traders underperform**: They sell low, miss recovery.

**If you held:**
- You're down 0.8% (almost breakeven)
- You learned the most valuable lesson
- You're ready for actual investing

## Comparison: What if Market Had Continued Down?

**Scenario: The decline continued to -40%**

```
Week 4: Down more, doesn't bounce
Week 5: Down to -35%
Week 6: Down to -40%
Week 7-8: Still down 40%

Your portfolio: From $25,000 down to $15,000

Now what?
- If you held: Down 40%, but positioned for recovery
- If you sold: Down 40% + regret + sitting in cash
```

Even in a -40% scenario, holding is typically better than selling, because:
1. You avoid locking in losses
2. You're positioned for recovery
3. You can contribute more during the decline
4. Math works in your favor on recovery

$15,000 up 50% = $22,500 (still down but much better than $15,000 selling panic + higher entry)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Holding through crashes is the key to wealth** - Every bear market since 1900 has recovered
2. **Panic selling is the real killer** - Not the crash itself
3. **Diversification provides psychological safety** - Bonds up, stocks down = you sleep better
4. **The recovery is always faster than you expect** - -26% to -0.8% in 4 weeks
5. **Next crash will test you again** - Now you know you can hold
