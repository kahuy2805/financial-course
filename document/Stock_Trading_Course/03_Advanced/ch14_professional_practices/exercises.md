## Exercise Set 7: Business Structure and Compliance

### Exercise 7.1: Choose Your Business Structure

```
BUSINESS STRUCTURE DECISION:

Annual trading income: $__________

Option 1: Sole Proprietor
Tax treatment: Schedule C (personal return)
Self-employment tax: Full 15.3%
Example: $50,000 profit
- Income tax: ~$12,000 (24% bracket)
- SE tax: ~$7,650
Total: ~$19,650 (39% effective rate)

Pros: ☐ Simple, minimal setup cost
Cons: ☒ No liability protection, high SE tax

Option 2: LLC (Single Member)
Tax treatment: Schedule C (or S-election)
Self-employment tax: Same as sole prop
Example: $50,000 profit
- Income tax: ~$12,000
- SE tax: ~$7,650
- LLC setup: ~$500-1,000/year
Total: ~$20,150 (40% effective rate)

Pros: ☒ Some liability protection
Cons: ☒ Still high SE tax, extra paperwork

Option 3: S-Corp Election
Tax treatment: Corporate with S-election
Self-employment tax: Only on wages, not distributions
Example: $50,000 profit
- Take $35,000 salary (SE tax): ~$4,950
- Take $15,000 distribution (no SE tax): $0
- Income tax on total: ~$12,000
- Accounting cost: ~$2,500
Total: ~$19,450 (39% effective rate, but more complex)

Pros: ☒ Significant tax savings at high income
Cons: ☒ More expensive accounting, quarterly filings

DECISION:
Annual income < $50,000: ☐ Sole Proprietor (simplest)
Annual income $50,000-100,000: ☐ LLC (balance)
Annual income > $100,000: ☐ S-Corp (tax savings exceed costs)

My choice: __________________________
Filing deadline: __________
Accountant consultant: __________________________
```
