## Exercise Set 1: Chart Type Recognition (Basic)

### Exercise 1.1: Identifying Chart Types

You see these three chart images. For each, identify the type:

**Image 1:** A simple line connecting closing prices, no volume bars
- Chart type: _______

**Image 2:** Vertical lines with small horizontal ticks on left and right
- Chart type: _______

**Image 3:** Colored rectangles with thin lines extending above/below
- Chart type: _______

### Exercise 1.2: Converting Between Chart Types

1. Draw a single day's candlestick for:
   - Open: $100
   - High: $110
   - Low: $95
   - Close: $108

2. Now draw the same data as a bar chart

3. Describe what a line chart would show for this day's data

### Exercise 1.3: Real-World Chart Reading

Go to TradingView.com:

1. Search for Apple (AAPL)
2. Switch between Line, Bar, and Candlestick views
3. Which view do you prefer? Why?
4. What information is clear in candlestick view that's hidden in line view?

## Exercise Set 2: Timeframe Analysis (Intermediate)

### Exercise 2.1: Multi-Timeframe Comparison

Pick a stock (suggest: MSFT, GOOGL, or TSLA):

1. Pull up a weekly chart
   - Is it in uptrend, downtrend, or sideways?
   - Describe the trend: _______________________

2. Pull up a daily chart
   - Is it in uptrend, downtrend, or sideways?
   - Describe the trend: _______________________

3. Pull up a 4-hour chart
   - Is it in uptrend, downtrend, or sideways?
   - Describe the trend: _______________________

**Analysis:**
- Do all three timeframes agree? (YES/NO)
- If not, which timeframe shows the "real" trend? _______
- Based on this, what trading action would you take?

### Exercise 2.2: Timeframe Mismatch Scenario

**Scenario:** You're looking at a 1-hour chart. It shows a strong uptrend, so you want to buy.

1. Before buying, what should you check? _______
2. Go check the daily chart. Is it in uptrend? (Check a real stock)
3. Is the 1-hour uptrend with or against the daily trend?
4. Based on this, should you buy? (Explain)

### Exercise 2.3: Choosing Your Timeframe

You want to be a swing trader (holding positions 2-5 days).

1. What timeframe should you use for primary analysis? _______
2. What timeframe for finding entry points? _______
3. What timeframe for managing the position? _______
4. Justify your answers.

## Exercise Set 3: Support and Resistance Identification (Intermediate)

### Exercise 3.1: Finding Support Levels

Find a stock that's been bouncing (not trending strongly).

1. Identify THREE support levels where price has bounced up
   - Support 1: $_______ (bounced _____ times)
   - Support 2: $_______ (bounced _____ times)
   - Support 3: $_______ (bounced _____ times)

2. Which support is strongest? (most bounces, highest volume)

3. Which support would you most confidently buy at?

4. What would be your stop loss for a buy at this level?

### Exercise 3.2: Finding Resistance Levels

Find the same stock (or a different one in uptrend).

1. Identify THREE resistance levels where price has bounced down
   - Resistance 1: $_______ (bounced _____ times)
   - Resistance 2: $_______ (bounced _____ times)
   - Resistance 3: $_______ (bounced _____ times)

2. Which resistance is strongest?

3. If you wanted to SHORT this stock, where would you enter?

### Exercise 3.3: Support/Resistance Breakdown

Find a stock where support or resistance HAS BROKEN.

1. What was the support/resistance level? $_______

2. Was it support or resistance? _______

3. How many times did it hold before breaking? _____

4. What happened to price after the break?
   - Did it reverse quickly? YES / NO
   - Did it continue in the breakout direction? YES / NO

5. If it was support that broke, what new level did price find?

### Exercise 3.4: Building a S/R Chart

Pick a stock and create your own S/R analysis:

1. Draw horizontal lines at all visible support levels
2. Draw horizontal lines at all visible resistance levels
3. Label each with the price
4. Rate each level: Strong, Moderate, or Weak (based on touches and volume)
5. Create a trading plan: Where would you buy/sell based on these levels?

## Exercise Set 4: Trend Line and Channel Analysis (Intermediate)

### Exercise 4.1: Drawing Uptrend Lines

Find a stock in a clear UPTREND:

1. Identify at least 3 rising lows (bottoms of bounces)
2. Draw an uptrend line connecting them
3. Extend the line into the future
4. Answer:
   - How many times has price bounced OFF this trend line? _____
   - Is the trend line strong (3+ touches)? YES / NO
   - What is the slope of the trend line (degrees)? _____ °

### Exercise 4.2: Drawing Downtrend Lines

Find a stock in a clear DOWNTREND:

1. Identify at least 3 falling highs (tops of bounces)
2. Draw a downtrend line connecting them
3. Extend the line into the future
4. Answer:
   - How many times has price bounced OFF this trend line? _____
   - Is the trend line strong? YES / NO
   - What is the slope? _____ °

### Exercise 4.3: Trading a Channel

Find a stock trading in a horizontal channel (bouncing between support and resistance):

1. Identify the support line (lower): $_______
2. Identify the resistance line (upper): $_______
3. Calculate the channel width: $_______ (height)

4. Create a trading plan:
   - "When price approaches support at $______, I will BUY at $____"
   - "When price approaches resistance at $______, I will SELL at $____"
   - "If I catch 5 bounces, I will profit $______ per trade"
   - "Total profit from 5 bounces: $______"

5. Monitor this channel for the next week. How many times did price bounce?

### Exercise 4.4: Channel Breakout Detection

Find a stock that HAS BROKEN OUT of a channel:

1. What were the channel boundaries?
   - Support: $_______
   - Resistance: $_______

2. Which direction did it break? (Up/Down)

3. Did volume INCREASE on the breakout? (Check the volume bar)

4. What happened after?
   - Did price continue in breakout direction?
   - How far did it move after breaking?

5. If you had recognized the breakout and traded it, what would your profit have been?

## Exercise Set 5: Volume Analysis (Intermediate)

### Exercise 5.1: Volume Confirmation

Find 5 examples of price moving with strong volume:

For each:
1. Did price move up or down?
2. What was the volume level? (High, Normal, Low)
3. Did the volume match the direction? (Bullish volume with up move? Bearish with down?)
4. Would you have traded based on this volume confirmation?

**Example Table:**

| Example | Price Move | Volume | Volume Type | Match? | Trade? |
|---------|-----------|--------|-------------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Up $2 | 5M | High | YES | YES |
| 2 | Down $1 | 500k | Low | NO | NO |

### Exercise 5.2: Identifying Weak Moves (Low Volume)

Find 3 examples of price moving on LOW volume:

For each:
1. What was the price move?
2. How low was the volume?
3. What happened to price after? (Did it reverse?)
4. Would you have traded this weak move?

**Learning:** Low volume moves often reverse. Avoid trading them.

### Exercise 5.3: Volume Divergence

Find a stock where price and volume DON'T AGREE:

**Example 1: Price Up, Volume Down**
1. Describe the price action
2. Describe the volume action
3. What does this divergence warn you about?
4. What happened next?

**Example 2: Price Down, Volume Down**
1. Describe the price action
2. Describe the volume action
3. What does this divergence warn you about?
4. What happened next? (Did price bounce up?)

### Exercise 5.4: Support Strength Based on Volume

Find a support level that has held 3+ times:

1. Support level: $_______
2. Volume on first bounce: _______ shares
3. Volume on second bounce: _______ shares
4. Volume on third bounce: _______ shares

**Analysis:**
- Is volume INCREASING or DECREASING at this support?
- What does this tell you about support strength?
- Would you be confident buying at this level?

## Exercise Set 6: Chart Pattern Recognition (Advanced)

### Exercise 6.1: Head and Shoulders

Find a completed head and shoulders pattern (or from historical data):

1. Measure the left shoulder peak: $_______
2. Measure the head peak: $_______
3. Measure the right shoulder peak: $_______
4. Identify the neckline: $_______

5. Calculate the pattern target:
   - Head height: $_______ - $_______ = $_______
   - Pattern target: $_______ - $_______ = $_______

6. Did price reach the target? (Check historical data)
   - If YES: How accurate was the pattern?
   - If NO: Why do you think it failed?

### Exercise 6.2: Double Top

Find a double top pattern (completed or forming):

1. First peak: $_______
2. Second peak: $_______
3. Neckline (valley between): $_______
4. Did price break below the neckline? (YES/NO)

If broken:
- How far did price fall?
- Did it hit the calculated target?
- What was your profit if you had shorted at the neckline?

### Exercise 6.3: Double Bottom

Find a double bottom pattern (completed or forming):

1. First bottom: $_______
2. Second bottom: $_______
3. Neckline/resistance (peak between): $_______
4. Did price break above the neckline? (YES/NO)

If broken:
- How far did price rise?
- Did it hit the calculated target?
- What was your profit if you had bought at the neckline?

### Exercise 6.4: Triangle

Find a triangle formation:

1. Start date of triangle: _______
2. Triangle height (initial range): $_______
3. Upper resistance line: $_______
4. Lower support line: $_______
5. Convergence point (where lines meet): _______

6. Prediction: Which direction will it break? (Up/Down/Uncertain)
7. When did it actually break? _______
8. Which direction? _______
9. How far did it move after breaking?

### Exercise 6.5: Pattern Accuracy Test

Choose 5 completed chart patterns from the past (on any stocks):

For each pattern:
- What type? (H&S, Double Top, Double Bottom, Triangle)
- Did it work? (Price reached target) YES / NO
- By what percentage?

**Summary:**
- Total patterns analyzed: _____
- Patterns that worked: _____
- Success rate: ______%

**Conclusion:** Are patterns reliable? (Write 2-3 sentences)

## Exercise Set 7: Moving Average Analysis (Advanced)

### Exercise 7.1: Adding Moving Averages

1. Go to your charting platform
2. Add three moving averages to a daily chart:
   - 20-day (short-term)
   - 50-day (medium-term)
   - 200-day (long-term)

3. Pick 5 different stocks and for each:
   - Describe the position of price vs each MA
   - Describe the alignment (spread apart or tangled?)
   - What trend is each stock in?

**Example:**

| Stock | Price vs 20 | Price vs 50 | Price vs 200 | Trend | Alignment |
|-------|----------|----------|-----------|-------|-----------|
| AAPL | Above | Above | Above | UP | Spread (good) |
| MSFT | Below | Below | Below | DOWN | Spread (good) |

### Exercise 7.2: MA as Support/Resistance

Find a stock where price bounces OFF a moving average (at least 2 times):

1. Which MA? (20/50/200) _______
2. How many bounces? _____
3. Is this MA acting as support or resistance?
4. On each bounce, was volume high? (Check volume bars)
5. Can you create a trading plan around this MA?
   - Buy at: $_______
   - Stop loss: $_______
   - Target: $_______

### Exercise 7.3: Golden Cross / Death Cross

Research historical examples:

**Golden Cross Example:**
1. Find a stock that had a golden cross (20-MA crossed above 200-MA) in the past
2. When did it happen? _______
3. What was the stock price? $_______
4. What happened in the next 3 months?
   - Price at 3 months: $_______
   - % gain/loss: _______%

**Death Cross Example:**
1. Find a stock that had a death cross (20-MA crossed below 200-MA)
2. When did it happen? _______
3. What was the stock price? $_______
4. What happened in the next 3 months?
   - Price at 3 months: $_______
   - % gain/loss: _______%

**Conclusion:** Do golden crosses usually lead to gains? Do death crosses lead to losses?

### Exercise 7.4: MA Alignment Signals

Monitor 3 stocks for one week:

For each stock, track daily:
- Are all three MAs aligned (same direction)? YES/NO
- Is price making new highs? YES/NO
- Is volume increasing? YES/NO

**Daily Log:**

| Stock | Day 1 | Day 2 | Day 3 | Day 4 | Day 5 |
|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| AAPL | UP/OK | UP/OK | DOWN | - | - |
| MSFT | - | - | - | - | - |
| TSLA | - | - | - | - | - |

**Analysis:** When all conditions align (MAs aligned, new highs, rising volume), does price continue higher?

## Exercise Set 8: Comprehensive Multi-Tool Analysis (Advanced)

### Exercise 8.1: Complete Chart Analysis

Choose ONE stock and perform a COMPLETE analysis:

**Part A: Trend Identification**
1. What is the overall trend on daily chart? (Up/Down/Sideways)
2. What is the trend on 4-hour chart?
3. Which timeframe shows the "real" trend?

**Part B: Support and Resistance**
1. Identify 2 support levels
2. Identify 2 resistance levels
3. Rate the strength of each (Weak/Moderate/Strong)

**Part C: Trend Lines and Channels**
1. Can you draw a trend line? (If in trend)
2. Or are there support/resistance lines forming a channel?
3. Is the pattern holding?

**Part D: Volume Analysis**
1. Has volume been high or low recently?
2. Did recent price moves have volume confirmation?
3. Is volume increasing or decreasing?

**Part E: Moving Averages**
1. Add 20/50/200 MAs
2. How are they aligned?
3. Is price above or below each MA?
4. Any Golden/Death crosses recently?

**Part F: Chart Patterns**
1. Are there any chart patterns forming or formed?
2. If yes, what type?
3. Where would be your entry if you traded it?

**Part G: Trading Decision**
Based on ALL of this analysis, answer:

1. What is the overall setup? (Strong buy/moderate buy/avoid/moderate sell/strong sell)
2. Where would you enter? $_______
3. Where would you place stop loss? $_______
4. Where would you place profit target? $_______
5. What is your risk/reward ratio?
6. Confidence level (1-10): _______
7. Why would/wouldn't you trade this?
