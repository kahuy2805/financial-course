## Section 1: What is the Stock Market? (Lesson 1.1)

### Question 1.1.1
Explain in 2-3 sentences why the stock market exists.

**Your Answer:** _______________________________

**Model Answer:** The stock market exists for two main reasons: (1) Companies need capital to grow and expand, which they raise by selling shares to investors, and (2) Individual investors want to build wealth by owning pieces of successful companies and sharing in their profits.

---

### Question 1.1.2
Sarah wants to start a business. She needs $100,000 but only has $25,000. Instead of borrowing from a bank, she decides to "go public" and sell shares. She divides her company into 100,000 shares and sells 75,000 at $1 each.

- How much capital did she raise? $75,000
- What percentage of her company does she still own? 25%
- What is the "market cap" of her company (value × shares)? $100,000

**Your Answers:**
- Capital raised: _______
- Percentage owned: _______
- Market cap: _______

---

### Question 1.1.3
What's the difference between the primary market and secondary market?

**Your Answer:** _______________________________

**Model Answer:** The primary market is where a company first sells shares to the public (IPO). The secondary market is where investors trade those shares among themselves afterward. Companies don't get money from secondary market trades.

---

### Question 1.1.4
You buy 100 shares of Apple at $150 per share.

- How much money did you spend? _______
- What fraction of Apple do you own? _______
- If Apple's market cap is $3 trillion and has 15 billion shares, what's Apple's stock price? _______

**Answers:**
- Spent: $15,000
- Own: 100/15,000,000,000 = 0.0000007%
- Stock price: $3,000,000,000,000 / 15,000,000,000 = $200/share

## Section 2: How Stock Exchanges Work (Lesson 1.2)

### Question 2.1
What's the main purpose of a stock exchange?

**Your Answer:** _______________________________

**Model Answer:** To create an organized, centralized marketplace where stocks can be bought and sold efficiently with fair pricing, liquidity, and transparent rules.

---

### Question 2.2
What are the key differences between NYSE and NASDAQ?

| Aspect | NYSE | NASDAQ |
|--------|------|--------|
| Founded | 1792 | 1971 |
| Trading Style | ? | Electronic |
| Typical Companies | ? | Tech-heavy |
| Market Cap | ~$28 trillion | ~$35 trillion |

**Your Answers for ?:**
- NYSE Trading Style: Physical floor + electronic
- NYSE Typical Companies: Large, established companies

---

### Question 2.3
Trace a stock order from placing it to settlement:

1. You place a _______
2. Your broker sends it to the _______
3. The exchange's _______ engine matches it
4. Trade _______ in milliseconds
5. Settlement completes in _______

**Answers:**
1. limit order (or market order)
2. stock exchange
3. matching
4. executes
5. 1-2 business days

---

### Question 2.4
Explain bid-ask spread:

You see Apple with:
- Bid: $149.95
- Ask: $150.05

If you want to BUY immediately, you pay: $_______
If you want to SELL immediately, you receive: $_______
The spread is: $_______

**Answers:**
- Buy: $150.05
- Sell: $149.95
- Spread: $0.10

---

### Question 2.5
True or False:

A) The stock exchange determines stock prices
**Answer:** False (supply/demand determines prices)

B) Trades happen instantly
**Answer:** True (milliseconds actually)

C) All stocks trade on both NYSE and NASDAQ
**Answer:** False (each company lists on one exchange)

D) You can trade stocks 24/7
**Answer:** False (regular hours 9:30 AM - 4 PM ET)

## Section 3: Key Market Participants (Lesson 1.3)

### Question 3.1
Match the participant to their description:

1. _____ Institutional Investor
2. _____ Broker
3. _____ Market Maker
4. _____ Retail Investor
5. _____ Exchange
6. _____ Analyst

A) Always ready to buy or sell to provide liquidity
B) Individual person investing personal money
C) Operates the trading platform and matching engine
D) Executes your buy/sell orders for a fee
E) Large organization managing billions of dollars
F) Researches companies and publishes recommendations

**Answers:** 1-E, 2-D, 3-A, 4-B, 5-C, 6-F

---

### Question 3.2
Why are institutional investors more powerful than retail investors?

**Your Answer:** _______________________________

**Model Answer:** Institutional investors manage billions of dollars and can move markets with single trades. When institutions buy/sell, prices move 2-5%. They also have better information and can influence corporate decisions.

---

### Question 3.3
A major analyst publishes a "BUY" rating for Tesla. What happens next?

**Your Answer:** _______________________________

**Model Answer:** Retail investors see the recommendation and want to buy. Buying pressure increases. Stock price rises. The analyst influenced market behavior through credibility and media attention.

---

### Question 3.4
Why would a company be concerned about what market makers do?

**Your Answer:** _______________________________

**Model Answer:** Market makers provide liquidity and tight bid-ask spreads. If they abandon a stock (rare), spreads widen and trading becomes harder. Companies want easy stock trading because it attracts investors.

### Question 4.4
A mutual fund charges 1.5% annual fee. An index fund charges 0.05% annual fee. Over 30 years investing $10,000:

What's the cost difference?

**Calculation:**
- 1.5% annual = $150 first year
- 0.05% annual = $5 first year
- Over 30 years with compound growth, the difference is massive (can be 30-50%+ of returns!)

## Section 5: Bull Markets vs Bear Markets (Lesson 1.5)

### Question 5.1
Define:

**Bull Market:** _______________________________

**Bear Market:** _______________________________

**Model Answers:**
- Bull Market: 20%+ price increase with positive sentiment
- Bear Market: 20%+ price decrease with negative sentiment

---

### Question 5.2
Historical Statistics - True or False:

A) Bull markets happen once per decade
**Answer:** False (about every 5 years)

B) Bear markets always recover
**Answer:** True (100% of the time historically)

C) Stocks are up 75% of the time
**Answer:** True

D) You can predict when bear markets occur
**Answer:** False (they're unpredictable)

---

### Question 5.3
You're a buy-and-hold investor. Your portfolio drops 30% (bear market).

What should you do?
A) Panic sell
B) Hold and stay invested
C) Move to cash
D) Buy more if you have cash

**Best Answer:** B or D (Hold, and buy more if possible)
**Worst Answer:** A (Panic selling locks in losses)

---

### Question 5.4
Historical bear market data:

| Year | Decline | Recovery Time |
|------|---------|----------------|
| 2008 | 57% | 4+ years |
| 2020 | 34% | 5 months |
| 2000-02 | 78% | 15 years |

What's the pattern?

**Model Answer:** Deeper declines sometimes take longer to recover, but all eventually fully recovered and went higher. Holding works.

---

### Question 5.5
You invested $10,000 in March 2008 (worst timing):

- Portfolio in Oct 2008: $5,000 (dropped 50%)
- Portfolio in Mar 2009: $4,500 (dropped 55%)
- Portfolio in Mar 2024: $50,000 (gained 400%+)

What's the lesson?

**Model Answer:** Even with the worst timing, holding through the bear market made enormous wealth. Don't panic sell.

### Question 6.3
True or False:

A) You should trade earnings releases in after-hours
**Answer:** False (wait for morning when spreads are tight)

B) Pre-market has the same spreads as regular session
**Answer:** False (pre-market spreads much wider)

C) The market is open on Thanksgiving
**Answer:** False (major holiday closure)

D) Overnight gaps can't be predicted
**Answer:** True (news breaks while market is closed)

---

### Question 6.4
Strategy for earnings release at 4:30 PM:

**Should you:**
A) Trade after-hours at 4:30 PM
B) Wait until 9:30 AM next morning
C) Set a pre-market alert

**Best Answer:** B (Wait until morning with tight spreads and clear head)

**Why not A?** After-hours spreads are wide, you'll overpay/undersell

**Why not C?** Pre-market is even worse than after-hours

---

### Question 6.5
You hold Apple stock and major economic data comes out 2 hours before market open.

What could happen?

A) Stock will gap at open (open at very different price)
B) You can react immediately in pre-market
C) Your stop-loss might execute at bad prices
D) All of the above

**Answer:** D (All of these are true - gaps happen, pre-market is risky, stop-losses execute poorly)
